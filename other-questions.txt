So here's an answer to the question:

Q: You talked about silly reasons for not making a puzzle game. What are some good reasons you've had for *not* making a puzzle game? Or are there any, is the moral of this talk that actually, maybe there aren't any good reasons for *not* making a puzzle game?

A: My personal number one good reason for not making a puzzle game is that I feel obligated to. I've got a bad habit of undervaluing silence, or of underappreciating silence... I'm slowly getting better at recognizing when a creative project would burn me out, versus when it'd be healing to push through discomfort. Listening to my early music, or playing through early attempts at level design... it was good skill-building, but the art itself never shakes the feeling of obligation.
